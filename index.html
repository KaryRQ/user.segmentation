User segmentation

Role: UX Research
Methods: Interviews, analysis of previous research, stakeholder iterations, segmentation definition, and communication mapping

Objective

The goal of this project was to define user profiles that could be used across all areas of the company to enable more effective communication campaigns, following the process described below.

Initial Approach

We started with a previous research study conducted by an agency, which identified 36 user profiles. However, most of them had overlapping characteristics. The objective was to reduce this number to a manageable set of profiles with the essential attributes needed by each business area.

Interviews

After analyzing the existing information, we carried out interviews with different internal teams responsible for sending communications to users. These sessions revealed that there was no unified process or coordination between areas, leading to inconsistencies. During the interviews, stakeholders also expressed their specific needs and expectations for how segmentation should support their work.

Development

With the insights collected from both the agency’s study and the interviews, we began refining the segmentation through weekly sessions with stakeholders. Each iteration allowed us to incorporate feedback, progressively reducing the number of segments while ensuring the profiles were useful and relevant for all involved areas.

Final Segmentation

Through this iterative process, we finalized four user profiles that captured the critical requirements of each business area. These became the foundation for sending targeted and consistent communications to end users.

Communication Map

With the segmentation defined, we proposed a communication map between the different areas. This ensured that users would not receive duplicate or irrelevant messages. As a result, call center complaints about email unsubscribes decreased by 18%, while email open rates increased significantly.
